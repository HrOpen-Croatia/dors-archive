DORS/CLUC

Archive of the Croatian free software conference, 1996–2026.

The live website at dorscluc.org has been retired. This is a static snapshot of every public edition of Dani otvorenih računarskih sustava and the Croatian Linux Users' Convention, collected from the original servers before they were decommissioned.

Modern era (2013–2026)

  • 2026

    The final edition. Call for speakers, sponsors, and the last programme before the conference wound down.

  • 2023

    Thirtieth DORS/CLUC, hosted at the Faculty of Electrical Engineering and Computing in Zagreb — the post-pandemic return.

  • 2022

    Flossing together since 1994. Full schedule, speakers, sponsors, and session videos.

  • 2020

    The virtual edition — Open Systems Days and Croatian Linux Users' Conference held online.

  • 2019

    26th edition. Talks, workshops, and the social programme around open systems in Croatia.

  • 2018

    25th edition — the silver anniversary. Speakers from across the free-software world.

  • 2017

    24th Open Systems Days. Schedule, speakers, and the annual hack-focused gathering.

  • 2016

    23rd edition. First year on the Django-backed site; full talk and speaker programme.

  • 2015

    22nd Open Systems Days, organised by HULK and HrOpen with the FER faculty.

  • 2014

    21st edition — the original WordPress-era site with talks, sponsors, and registration archives.

  • 2013

    20th edition — speakers, schedule, and call-for-papers.

Download the whole archive

The complete archive — every page, image, PDF and presentation — is available as a single compressed tarball for offline use, mirroring, or pulling into your own editor to fix errors and submit a PR: 

curl -O https://archive.dorscluc.org/dorscluc-archive-latest.tar.zst
tar --zstd -xf dorscluc-archive-latest.tar.zst

~985 MB compressed, ~1.1 GB extracted. Regenerated from source periodically; the latest symlink always points at the current build.

Evergreen pages (Code of Conduct, past-conferences index, contact) live at /main/. Classic-era sites were served from close.open.hr and rendered by HULK and HrOpen long before the dorscluc.org domain existed.
